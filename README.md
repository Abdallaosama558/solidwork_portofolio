Coke Bottle: Demonstrates advanced surface modeling, lofts, guide curves, and a fully threaded bottle neck.

Headphones: Features adjustable headband, detailed ear cups/cushions, and swivel hinge joints (utilizing sub-assemblies).

Propellers: Features twisted airfoil geometry suitable for rotation studies or Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) analysis.

# ‚ú® Modeling Standards and Practices
The following standards were rigorously applied to ensure model quality, maintainability, and professional documentation:

Parametric Design Intent: Models are built for scalable modification, with dimensions linked to global variables where beneficial.

Sketching: All sketches are fully defined and constrained (black lines only) to prevent unintended geometry changes.

Organization: Clear naming conventions are used consistently for features, sketches, parts, and configurations.

Materials: Realistic materials are assigned to all parts (e.g., ABS, Aluminum, PET, Steel) to support rendering and simulation.

Assembly Techniques: Assemblies rely on standard and mechanical mates, including:

Concentric, Coincident, Parallel, and Lock mates.

Effective use of sub-assemblies for large systems (e.g., headphones, mouse, drawer rails).
